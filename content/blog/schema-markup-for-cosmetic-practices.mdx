If you've ever wondered why one practice's pages show up in search with rich results — star
ratings, FAQs, breadcrumbs — while yours appear as plain blue links, the answer is usually
schema markup. It's one of the highest-leverage, least-glamorous things you can do for a
cosmetic practice's SEO.

## What schema actually is

Schema (or structured data) is a small block of machine-readable code, written in a vocabulary
called Schema.org, that tells search engines what a page *means* rather than just what it says.
A human reads "Dr Smith performs rhinoplasty in Sydney"; a search engine reads a `Physician`
node, a `MedicalProcedure`, and a service area — but only if you tell it.

## The types that matter for a cosmetic practice

You don't need dozens. A focused handful covers most of the value:

- **Organization / MedicalBusiness** — who the practice is, where, and how to contact it
- **Physician** — the surgeon or doctor, their credentials and specialties
- **MedicalProcedure** — each procedure you offer, on its own page
- **FAQPage** — question-and-answer blocks, which can earn rich results
- **BreadcrumbList** — your page hierarchy, which tidies how URLs appear in search
- **Article** — for each post in your journal or blog

## How to use it without overdoing it

Schema rewards accuracy and punishes exaggeration. A few principles:

1. **Only mark up what's genuinely on the page.** Inventing reviews or FAQs in schema that a
   user can't see is against the guidelines and risks a penalty.
2. **Keep one set of facts.** Your name, address and phone number should match exactly across
   schema, your site and your listings.
3. **Let it reference itself.** Give key nodes stable identifiers so the practice, its pages
   and its articles connect into one coherent graph.

> Good structured data isn't a trick. It's just telling the truth in a format machines can read.

## The honest caveat

Schema doesn't make a slow, thin, untrustworthy site rank. It helps a *good* site be understood
and presented well. Treat it as the finishing layer on solid foundations — content patients
actually need, on a fast and accessible site — and it earns its keep.
