## The client

Dr Yalda Jamali is a cosmetic doctor with a decade of clinical experience and a
growing public profile.

She holds a master's degree in dermatology, and her profile spans press features,
podcast appearances, brand partnerships and teaching. When she moved her practice
from the UK to Sydney a few years ago, her existing website couldn't make the move
with her — it was built for a different market, a different regulatory framework and
a different business. What remained of her online presence lived in fragments: a
social profile here, a press feature there, a booking link somewhere else again.
Nothing she owned in Australia brought it together.

![Dr Yalda Jamali homepage — a full-screen portrait of Dr Yalda beneath her serif wordmark](/assets/work/dr-yalda-jamali/home-hero.jpg "The homepage — a cinematic, film-still first impression")

## The brief

The website had to work for two very different audiences.

Patients, whether researching a doctor for the first time or booking a return visit
across her two Sydney clinics. And journalists and brand partners, who needed a
credible press page and a clear route to her PR manager.

It also had to be built for Australian cosmetic advertising guidance from day one.
The rules shape what a regulated practice can publish, so trust would be earned
through professionalism and evidence rather than promotion.

## What we did

We designed the site the way Dr Yalda practises: consultation-led, evidence-first,
nothing overclaimed.

A cinematic visual language of full-bleed film stills, a quiet serif and a warm,
understated palette sets a professional, trustworthy tone from the first frame.

![The services page, showing a clinical focus list: fine lines and wrinkles, skin laxity, uneven skin tone, facial balancing, volume and collagen loss, preventative skin health](/assets/work/dr-yalda-jamali/services-focus.jpg "The services page — a clinical focus list, not a treatment menu")

- A services page built around clinical focus areas (skin quality, facial balancing,
  preventative skin health) rather than a treatment menu, with specifics kept for the
  consultation room where they belong
- Social proof handled in line with national guidelines: testimonials from media and
  brand partners rather than patient reviews
- A dedicated media hub covering features, podcasts and event work, with enquiries
  routed straight to her PR agency
- Credentials given room to breathe: qualifications, fellowship, teaching and AHPRA
  registration presented plainly on the about page
- Appointments across both clinics, plus short video consultations with their limits
  stated honestly
- An editorial engine, *Notes from the practice*, so her educator's voice compounds
  into search visibility over time

![The media page, listing press features across The Memo, Elle Australia and Mamamia](/assets/work/dr-yalda-jamali/media-features.jpg "The media page — expert commentary across national press")

Under the surface, the site is built for search: fast static pages and a
structured-data graph that describes the practice the way search engines read it,
with Dr Yalda as a person, her AHPRA registration as a verifiable identifier, and
both clinic locations linked as places she works.

> In a category that sells transformation, restraint is the trust signal. The site
> never overclaims; it behaves the way the practice does.

![The appointments page — in-person consultations at two Sydney clinics, Paddington and Balgowlah](/assets/work/dr-yalda-jamali/appointments.jpg "The appointments page — two clinics, one clear booking path")

![The video consultation section — a phone mockup beside a plain list of what a video call can and can't cover](/assets/work/dr-yalda-jamali/appointments-phone.jpg "Short video consultations, with their limits stated plainly")

## The result

A single, professional home that reads like the doctor herself: credentialled,
considered and honest about what can and can't be promised.

Patients arrive at a consultation better informed, media enquiries reach the right
inbox first time, and every claim on the site is one the practice can stand behind.
