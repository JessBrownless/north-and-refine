Dr Elizabeth Hawkes is a consultant ophthalmic and oculoplastic surgeon in London: a
Tatler Top Doctor, an award winner, a journal advisor and one of a small number of UK
surgeons who operate on eyes and eyelids exclusively. She practises at the Cadogan
Clinic in Chelsea and The London Lauriston Clinic, and the national press regularly
calls on her for expert comment. The authority was never in question. The brief was
to make sure the patients searching for her expertise actually found it.

*[insert screenshot: homepage hero — the serif headline beside Dr Hawkes' portrait]*

## The brief

Patients considering eyelid surgery research harder than almost any other cosmetic
patient, and they search in two ways: by procedure when they know what they want
("blepharoplasty London"), and by symptom when they don't (heavy lids, dark circles,
puffy eyes). The website had to meet both kinds of search, answer them properly, and
carry the weight of Dr Hawkes' credentials without shouting about them.

## What we did

We built the site around how patients actually search, then let the evidence of her
work do the persuading.

*[insert screenshot: the procedure navigation — oculoplastic services, aesthetic services and conditions]*

- A procedure-led architecture: a dedicated, in-depth page for every oculoplastic and
  aesthetic service, each one targeting the exact queries patients use
- Condition pages that meet symptom-first searches, reaching patients before they
  know the name of the procedure they need
- Authority made visible: awards, Tatler recognition, national press features and
  hundreds of patient reviews, woven through the site rather than hidden on a page
- A before-and-after gallery presented with care and proper consent — in this market,
  real results are the strongest evidence there is
- An editorial engine answering the questions patients ask about eyes, from dark
  circles to under-eye puffiness, compounding search visibility with every post
- Technical SEO throughout: fast static pages, structured data and per-page metadata
  tuned to the target queries

*[insert screenshot: the reviews and recognition sections — awards, Tatler listings and patient ratings]*

> Rankings aren't won with tricks. They're won by answering a patient's questions
> more thoroughly than anyone else on the page.

## The result

Top-three Google positions across the keywords the practice targets, including the
procedure searches that matter most to it. The site now works as the clinic's
patient-acquisition engine: prospective patients arrive already reassured by the
reviews, the press and the results, and consultations begin from trust rather than
from scratch.
